Service (Service Layer)

Description:
The service layer contains business logic and transaction boundaries. Services coordinate repositories and other components to enforce business rules, perform validations not appropriate for controllers, and manage application workflows.

Working:
- Controller invokes service methods with DTOs or mapped domain objects.
- Services execute business rules, call repositories (CRUD), and manage transactions (e.g., @Transactional).
- Services map entities to response DTOs (or vice versa), call external APIs, and handle retries, caching, or scheduling where needed.
- Errors are translated into domain exceptions; controllers map them to HTTP responses or global handlers.

Notes:
Keep services focused and testable; prefer small methods and composition. Avoid embedding HTTP or persistence concerns into controllers—services should own transactions and business consistency.
